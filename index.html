Australia's Gambling Addiction
Australia is the world's biggest loser in gambling losses. In 2017, it ranked first in the world for the amount of money lost through gambling.

One of the most important indicator of tracking how prominent gambling is, is by analysing a demographics' gambling expenditure per capita.

Gambling expenditure refers to the net loss of a gambler or net profit for the house over a fiscal year.

The following visualisation aims to evaluate Australia's gambling tendencies with a focus on Victoria.

Overview of Australia


Over time, Australia's gambling expenditure per capita has consistenly risen as the years go by. The only exception to this growth was when COVID-19 lockdown was implemented which caused venues to be closed. It has since recovered.


Methods of gambling can come in many shapes and form. Gaming Machines such as slot machines commonly available at locations such as clubs, pubs and casinos have dominated the Australian market due to ease of use and availability.
Where do we stand?


Victoria is the second highest state in gambling expenditure per capita in Australia. Below is a breakdown of average expenditure across different council regions (LGA). Based on the data, the closer you are to the middle or outer edges of Victoria then the higher the expenditure.


There are various factors which could influence the expenditure of a region. As seen below, there is an inverse relationship between number of adults per venue, which shows how saturated the venue in an LGA is, and the number of machines available. So an LGA with venues less saturated and higher machine count leads to higher expenditure.


Period of high unemployment could potentially affect gambling tendencies. As seen below, an increase in unemployment rate likely led to a decreased gambling expenditure.


From 2014 to 2019, there has been a slight increase in people choosing to not gamble. Overall, there is no significant changes in gambler type.
Public Perception of Gambling


According to a survey on what people think of gambling, collected by the Victorian Government, about a quarter believe that gambling is unacceptable regardless of how much is spent. Roughly 75% believes that gambling is acceptable, depending on how much is spent within a month.


In the same survey, roughly 73% believe that adolescents are being overexposed to gambling materials. This suggests that gambling has become such a normal thing in Victorian's lives, but people are still concerned over the amount of exposure available to the public, especially to the impressionable youth.
